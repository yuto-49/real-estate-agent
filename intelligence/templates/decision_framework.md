# Investment Decision Framework

## When Do People Buy?

### Life Event Triggers
The biggest purchase drivers are life changes: marriage, children, job relocation, caring for aging parents. Buyers who deferred purchases are returning because life milestones cannot be postponed indefinitely.

### Rate Floor Psychology
People buy when they believe rates have hit a new normal. With 30-year fixed rates averaging around 6.3%, buyers previously priced out are returning.

### Rent-vs-Buy Parity
When monthly mortgage cost approaches local rent, the incentive to buy spikes. In many 2026 Midwest markets, buying is becoming more attractive as rental prices stay high while home price growth moderates.

## Why Do They Invest?

### Forced Savings and Equity Growth
A mortgage payment is a forced savings mechanism. Even at modest 2-3% annual appreciation, a home hedges against inflation.

### Tax Advantages
Key mechanisms: depreciation (non-cash expense reducing taxable income), deductions (interest, property taxes, maintenance), and 1031 exchanges (sell and reinvest without immediate capital gains).

### Cash Flow and Passive Income
A growing permanent-renter population creates sustained rental demand. Evaluate properties by cap rate and cash-on-cash return.

### Portfolio Diversification
Real estate has low correlation with equities. Physical property provides a tangible asset class when markets are volatile.
